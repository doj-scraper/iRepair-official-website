## Logo direction

1. `Create a custom logo mark for iRepair Technologies using a simple wave-signal icon instead of a generic wrench or phone. Use 3 clean signal bands or arcs that suggest connection, reliability, and speed. Pair it with a compact wordmark, with a clean wholesale-tech feel, bay-blue and teal palette, pale-yellow accent, minimal but not boring, no gradients that feel generic, no app-icon cliché, no text inside the icon, no watermark`

## Brand pack prompt

`Build a compact brand pack for iRepair Technologies, a wholesale phone-parts storefront for repair shops. Direction: old-school internet meets clean modern wholesale hardware. Visual tone should feel trustworthy, a little nostalgic, and practical. Core palette: bay blue, muted teal, pale yellow, soft white, charcoal. Typography should be bold and readable with a slightly editorial feel, not generic startup sans. Logo should use a unique custom wave-signal icon that hints at connection, speed, and service, not a wrench, battery, or phone silhouette. Include: one primary logo lockup, one icon-only mark, one homepage hero image style, one catalog card image style, one announcement marquee style, and one short brand voice guide. Imagery should use real-world repair shop scenes, organized inventory, clean workbenches, friendly people, and candid lighting. Avoid anything overly glossy, corporate, neon, or stock-photo generic. Keep everything web-ready, practical, and coherent across desktop and mobile.`
