# iRepair Connect Brand Sheet

## Brand Summary

iRepair Connect is a wholesale phone-parts storefront for repair shops. The brand should feel practical, trustworthy, and a little nostalgic without looking dated. Think old-school internet utility with clean modern execution.

## Brand Personality

- Direct
- Friendly
- Shop-floor practical
- Organized
- Reliable
- Slightly retro

## Positioning

- Wholesale only, not retail
- Built for independent repair shops
- Fast local fulfillment
- QC-tested inventory
- Simple, no-fluff commerce

## Logo Direction

- Use a custom wave-signal mark rather than a generic wrench, battery, or phone icon.
- The icon should suggest connection, speed, and signal strength.
- Best direction: 3 clean arcs or bands, like a stylized radio or cellphone signal.
- Keep the mark ownable and simple enough to work as a favicon, header logo, and app icon.
- Pair it with a compact wordmark for the full lockup.

## Color System

Use the existing site palette as the source of truth:

- Bay blue: primary base color
- Muted teal: action and brand accent
- Pale yellow: highlight / hairline accent
- Soft white: background surface
- Charcoal: readable text and contrast

Color feel:

- Clean, not neon
- Warm enough to feel human
- Technical without looking corporate

## Typography

- Headlines: bold, confident, slightly editorial
- Body: plain, readable, and direct
- Labels: small caps or tracked uppercase for system-like utility

Avoid:

- Generic startup sans energy
- Overly decorative display fonts
- Thin light weights for important copy

## Imagery Direction

Use real-world, human, practical images:

- Houston or Texas-adjacent scenes are welcome and encouraged when they feel authentic.
- Repair shop workbenches
- Organized parts trays
- Candid technicians
- Friendly storefront moments
- Inventory shelves and shipping prep

Lighting:

- Natural or soft warehouse light
- Clean shadows
- Slightly warm, not glossy
- Local cues like Houston skylines, industrial textures, Gulf-coast light, and Texas warehouse environments can reinforce pride of place without feeling touristy.

Composition:

- Wide for hero and marquee
- Square for catalog cards
- Leave space for overlays and crop flexibility

## UI Language

- Structured and orderly
- Cards should feel consistent, not decorative for decoration’s sake
- Rounded containers can be used sparingly for emphasis
- Use clear separation between sections
- Prefer restrained motion over flashy animation

## Voice

Tone should sound:

- Confident
- Helpful
- Short and practical

Tone should avoid:

- Hype
- Corporate jargon
- Salesy filler

Examples:

- `Wholesale only`
- `QC tested`
- `Same-day dispatch`
- `Bulk pricing`
- `Built for repair shops`

## Do

- Keep the visual system cohesive across header, catalog, and marketing sections
- Use the wave-signal icon consistently
- Keep imagery practical and believable
- Use the brand colors with discipline

## Don't

- Don't use the wrench icon as the primary mark
- Don't use neon gradients or cyberpunk styling
- Don't lean into generic SaaS aesthetics
- Don't overcomplicate the logo mark
- Don't use stock photos that feel staged or over-polished
- Don't make Houston cues feel like generic travel postcards

## Short Creative Brief

Build a brand that feels like a trustworthy repair-industry utility with old-school internet charm. The system should be clean, organized, and memorable, with a custom wave-signal logo, bay-blue and teal color language, pale-yellow accents, and practical imagery from real repair and warehouse environments.

## 1. Logo Generation Prompt

`Design a custom logo for iRepair Connect, a wholesale phone-parts storefront for repair shops. Use a unique wave-signal icon, inspired by radio waves or cellphone signal bars, but make it ownable and minimal. The mark should feel like connection, speed, reliability, and signal strength. Avoid generic wrench, battery, or phone silhouettes. Build a compact lockup with the icon on the left and the wordmark on the right. Style: old-school internet utility meets clean modern wholesale brand. Palette: bay blue, muted teal, pale yellow accents, soft white, charcoal. Keep the icon simple enough for favicon use, but distinctive enough to feel branded. No watermark, no mockup, no text inside the icon, no overly corporate or neon style.`

## 2. Favicon / SVG Concept

- Shape: a square or rounded-square base with a clean wave-signal glyph inside.
- Icon idea: 3 arcs rising upward, or 3 horizontal signal bands, with a subtle circuit-like cut or break to imply repair and connectivity.
- Geometry: flat, symmetrical, and readable at 16px.
- Palette: primary bay blue background, white or soft-cream icon, pale-yellow accent as a tiny underline or highlight if needed.
- Feel: friendly, technical, and slightly retro.
- Avoid: tiny tool shapes, overly detailed gradients, and anything that collapses into a generic app icon.

Suggested SVG direction:

```text
Square canvas
Rounded background
Three curved signal arcs or stepped wave lines
Small accent stroke near the base
Minimal wordless icon
```

## 3. Homepage Art Direction Note

The homepage should feel like a modern wholesale storefront with a bit of old-web personality. Replace generic card-heavy marketing blocks with stronger, more memorable elements:

- Use one strong hero image that feels real, not staged.
- Use a scrolling marquee or bulletin strip for announcements, promos, and shop-floor updates.
- Let Houston-local photography and industrial textures carry the hero when possible.
- Keep the catalog and product imagery practical, organized, and candid.
- Let the visual system breathe with clear section breaks and less boxy repetition.
- Favor utility and rhythm over polished startup gloss.

The page should feel like a trusted shop-floor hub, not a SaaS landing page. The best reference point is an organized warehouse bulletin board blended with a clean retail homepage.
